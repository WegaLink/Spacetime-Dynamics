
A field installation connects multiple MCUs with a total of 200m Cat-7 outdoor cable S FTP AWG 23/1 in a ring topology with equal distances using one incoming and one outgoing RJ45 connector on each MCU node. Power and communication shall run on one of the 4 pairs of twisted wires in the network cable. One master MCU shall inject power for other MCUs and shall initiate communication to them. What electronic circuitry concept would best support described system? What electronic components can be used to realize the circuitry? What would be the maximal recommended number of MCUs in the 200m ring cable if the master MCU receives power from a 12V/2A PoE splitter from a PoE switch and if the total power consumption of a MCU node circuit would be about 2 watts? What would be expected bandwidth when the master MCU talks to one of the slave MCUs?

can the concept be adjusted to have exactly the same circuitry on master and slave while obviously only one MCU will get the 12V/2A power connected from a PoE switch?

Thanks for all information regarding power+data connectivity between nodes on one pair of wires in a network cable. Next the electronics for the actual node functionality should be discussed. Each node will have a phase controlled 600 MHz local oscillator and 3 phase comparator circuits AD8302. The three remaining pairs of wires will be used as follows to forward the signal from the local oscillator to adjacent nodes. One cable pair will forward the oscillator signal from adjacent node on the „incoming“ side to own node. The own oscillator signal of a node will be forwarded on the same pair of wires to the adjacent node on the „outgoing“ side. Furthermore, those signals will be sent back on a next pair of wires to the respective transmitting nodes. The fourth pair of wires will be used to sent a signal to the incoming side. This should be software selected either the signal from own local oscillator or alternatively the oscillator signal received from adjacent node on the outgoing side. Two phase comparators determine the phase difference between own local oscillator and the oscillators on the incoming and outgoing side. The third phase comparator determines phase variations between the local oscillator and the returned signal from own oscillator on the outgoing side. The output signal of the phase comparator will be used to synchronize the phases of all oscillators to the oscillator on the master node. The master node shall be synchronized to a GPS signal. Only the low frequency components < 1 mHz should be used for synchronizing the oscillators. The higher frequencies of the AD8302 phase output upto the limit (30 MHz) should be evaluated by a logarithmic detector. Furthermore, that signal will be sampled by a ADC of the MCU as well as also the comparator’s gain signal. What would be a suitable electronic design for described functionality and what components could be used to get reliable phase signals registered by the MCU down to 0.1 degree or better for the phase difference? What general hints and recommendations can be given for the system?
